[L0238] What location information do Gemini Apps collect, why, and how is it used?
[L0239] Location data is always collected if you use Gemini Apps so that they can provide you with a response that is relevant to your query.
[L0240] For example, to respond to prompts like "What's the weather?", Gemini Apps need to know your location.
[L0241] Where Gemini Apps get location data & how its used The source of the location data Gemini Apps collect varies.
[L0242] By default, Gemini Apps use the general area from your IP address or the Home or Work addresses in your Google Account to give relevant responses to prompts like “What’s the weather?”
[L0243] With your permission, Gemini Apps also process precise location data from your device.
[L0244] For example, Gemini Apps can use your precise location to more accurately respond to prompts like “where is the closest coffee shop to me?” Gemini may also receive precise
[L0245] location data from another Google service, like Google Maps, even if the precise location setting in the Google app is off
[L0246] Google uses your location data, including your precise location data, for the purposes and based on the legal grounds described in the Gemini Apps Privacy Notice.
[L0247] Learn more about location data at g.co/privacypolicy/location   .
[L0248] Gemini Apps may share your precise location data with another Google service, like Google Maps, to fulfill your request.
[L0249] The Google service that gets your location data uses it consistent with the Google Privacy Policy .
[L0250] Gemini may receive your precise location from another Google service, like Google Maps, to fulfill your request even if the precise location setting in the Google app is off.
[L0251] How location data is stored
[L0252] Precise location that is processed with your permission from your device or browser is not stored in your Gemini Apps Activity.
[L0253] Location data that Gemini Apps process is coarsened to a general area before it’s stored with your Gemini Apps Activity. A general area is larger than 3 sq kilometers, and has at least 1000 users so that the general area of your prompt does not identify you, helping to protect your privacy. This means that a general area is typically much larger than 3 sq kilometers outside of cities.
